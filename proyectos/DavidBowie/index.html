DAVID BOWIE

He was a figure in popular music for over five decades, regarded by critics and musicians as an innovator, particularly for his work in the 1970s. His career was marked by reinvention and visual presentation, his music and stagecraft significantly influencing popular music.

English singer, songwriter, actor and record producer.
David Robert Jones (8 January 1947 – 10 January 2016)

Timeline of David Bowie:
    1947 - Born in Brixton, South London
    1953 - Moved with his family to the suburb of Bromley
    1955 - Attended Burnt Ash Junior School.
    1958 - Went to Bromley Technical High School.
    1961 - Enthusiasm for modern jazz led his mother to give him a plastic alto saxophone. Soon after he started to receive lessons from a local musician.
    1962 - Received a serious injury at school when his friend George Underwood punched him in the left eye during a fight over a girl. He was left with faulty depth perception and a permanently dilated pupil, which gave a false impression of a change in the iris' colour
    1962 - Formed his first band, The Konrads, at the age of 15. Playing guitar-based rock and roll.
    1963-1967 - Leaves the technical school, he informed his parents of his intention to become a pop star. He joined and left several bands, The Konrads, The King Bees, The Manish Boys, The Lower Third, Riot Squad, The Velvet Underground; after five unsuccessful debut releases. Dissatisfied with his stage name as Davy Jones, Bowie renamed himself.
    1967 - Release of solo single, "The Laughing Gnome" failed to chart until, 1973 it reached number 6.
    1967 - Met dancer Lindsay Kemp and enrolled in his dance class at the London Dance Centre. Bowie became immersed in the creation of personae to present to the world. Satirising life in a British prison.
    1968 - Kemp choreographed a dance scene for a BBC play The Pistol Shot in the Theatre 625 series, and used Bowie with a dancer, Hermione Farthingale. The pair began dating, and moved into a London flat together.
    1969 - Bowie and Farthingale break up when she went to Norway to take part in a film, Song of Norway. This made an impact on him, and several songs, such as 'Letter to Hermione' and 'Life on Mars?' and for the video accompanying "Where Are We Now?
    1969 - 'Space Oddity' was released five days ahead of the Apollo 11 launch, and reached the top five in the UK
    1969 - Met Angela Barnett in April 1969. They married within a year.
    1970 - Bowie's third album, 'The Man Who Sold the World', which contained references to schizophrenia, paranoia and delusion.
    1971 - 'Hunky Dory', a song written for his son, Duncan Zowie Haywood Jones, born on 30 May. The album explored more serious themes, and found Bowie paying unusually direct homage to his influences with "Song for Bob Dylan", "Andy Warhol", and "Queen Bitch"
    1972 - Dressed in a striking costume, his hair dyed reddish-brown, Bowie launched his stage show 'Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars.
    1973 - 'Aladdin Sane' topped the UK chart, his first number one album which spawned the UK top five singles 'The Jean Genie' and 'Drive-In Saturday'
    1974 - Moved to the US and releases album 'Diamond dogs'. The album went to number one in the UK, creating hits 'Rebel Rebel' and 'Diamond Dogs', and at number five in the US.
    1976 - Album release 'Station to Station' introduced a new Bowie persona, "The Thin White Duke"
    1977 - The album 'Low' was released to be the start of the 'Berlin Trilogy'. The second of the trilogy, 'Heroes', incorporated pop and rock to a greater extent. Like 'Low', 'Heroes' evinced the zeitgeist of the Cold War
    1979 - 'The Lodger' was released. A complex mixture of new wave and world music. A concept album that portrays the Lodger as a homeless wanderer, shunned and victimized by life's pressures and technology. Lodger reached number 4 in the UK and number 20 in the US. It yielded the UK hit singles 'Boys Keep Swinging' and 'DJ'.
    1980 - Bowie and Angela initiated divorce proceedings, and after months of court battles the marriage was ended. Within the same year 'Scary Monsters (And Super Creeps)' was released. It produced the number one hit 'Ashes to Ashes'. It hit numberone on the UK charts. Bowie opened a three-month run on Broadway on.
    1981 - Bowie paired with Queen for a one-off single release, 'Under Pressure'. The duet was a hit, becoming Bowie's third UK number one single
    1983 - Reached his peak of popularity and commercial success with 'Let's Dance'. The album went platinum in both the UK and the US. Its three singles became top twenty hits in both countries, where its title track reached number one. 'Modern Love' and 'China Girl' each made number two in the UK. Let's Dance was followed by the Serious Moonlight Tour
    1984 - 'Tonight', another dance-oriented album, found Bowie collaborating with Tina Turner and, once again, Iggy Pop.
    1985 - Performed at Wembley for Live Aid. During the event, the video for a fundraising single was premièred, Bowie's duet with Mick Jagger. "Dancing in the Street" quickly went to number one on release. The same year, Bowie worked with the Pat Metheny Group to record 'This Is Not America' for the soundtrack of The Falcon and the Snowman. Released as a single, the song became a top 40 hit in the UK and US
    1986 - Appeared as Jareth, the Goblin King, in the Jim Henson film 'Labyrinth', for which he wrote five songs
    1989 - Bowie shelved his solo career, retreating to the relative anonymity of band membership for the first time since the early 1970s. A hard-rocking quartet, Tin Machine came into being after Bowie began to work experimentally with guitarist Reeves Gabrels
    1990 - David and Somali-born supermodel Iman were introduced by a mutual friend.They married in 1992
    1992 - Appeared at The Freddie Mercury Tribute Concert, following the Queen singer's death the previous year. As well as performing 'Heroes' and 'All the Young Dudes', he was joined on 'Under Pressure' by Annie Lennox. Four days later, Bowie and Iman were married in Switzerland.
    1995 - 'Outside' release. The album achieved UK and US chart success, and yielded three top 40 UK singles. Bowie chose Nine Inch Nails as his 'Outside' Tour.
    1996 - Bowie was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
    1997 - On 7 January Bowie celebrated his half century with a 50th birthday concert at Madison Square Garden. Within the same year he also performed on the BBC's Children in Need charity single 'Perfect Day', which reached number one in the UK.
    1998 - Launched his own Internet service provider, BowieNet. Subscribers to the dial-up service were offered exclusive content. The service was closed by 2006.
    1999 - Created the soundtrack for Omikron, a computer game in which he and Iman also appeared as characters.
    2000 - Made his second appearance at the Glastonbury Festival in England, playing 30 years after his first. Bowie and Iman's daughter was born on 15 August.
    2001 - Opened the Concert for New York City, a charity event to benefit the victims of the 11 September attacks, with a minimalist performance of Simon & Garfunkel's 'America', followed by a full band performance of 'Heroes'.
    2002 - Saw the release of 'Heathen', and, during the second half of the year, the Heathen Tour
    2003 - Suffered chest pain while performing at the Hurricane Festival in Scheeßel, Germany. The pain was later diagnosed as an acutely blocked coronary artery, requiring an emergency angioplasty in Hamburg
    2004 - In the years following his recuperation from the heart attack, Bowie reduced his musical output, making only one-off appearances on stage and in the studio. He sang in a duet of his 1972 song "Changes" with Butterfly Boucher for the animated film Shrek 2.
    2006 - Awarded the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award on 8 February. In April, he announced, "I'm taking a year off—no touring, no albums."He performed again in November, alongside Alicia Keys, at the Black Ball, a New York benefit event for Keep a Child Alive, a performance marking the last time Bowie performed his music on stage.
    2007 - Was chosen to curate the High Line Festival, selecting musicians and artists for the Manhattan event, including electronic pop duo Air, surrealist photographer Claude Cahun and English comedian Ricky Gervais.
    2013 - Album release 'The Next Day'. Bowie's first studio album in a decade.
    An exhibition of Bowie artefacts, called "David Bowie Is", was organized by the V&A Museum in London. The London exhibit was visited by 311,956 people, making it one of the most successful exhibitions ever staged at the museum.
    2014 - Becomes the oldest recipient of a Brit Award in the ceremony's history when he wins the award for Best British Male, which is collected on his behalf by Kate Moss wearing his Ziggy Stardust costume.
    2015 - Bowie’s musical Lazarus debuted in New York. His last public appearance was at opening night of the production
    2016 - On Bowie's 69th birthday, studio album 'Blackstar' is released and met with critical acclaim. Just two days later, he died after a battle with liver cancer, aged 69. The day following his death, online viewing of Bowie's music skyrocketed, breaking the record for Vevo's most viewed artist in a single day. 15 January, Blackstar debuted at number one on the UK Albums Chart, nineteen of his albums were in the UK Top 100 Albums Chart, and thirteen singles were in the UK Top 100 Singles Chart. Blackstar also debuted at number one on album charts around the world.
